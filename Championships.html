Egyptian Cup

The Egyptian Cup, also known as the Egypt Cup, is the premier knockout football tournament in Egypt. It is organized by the Egyptian Football Association (EFA) and involves clubs from various divisions of Egyptian football. Here are some key points about the Egyptian Cup: 1. **Format:** The Egyptian Cup follows a knockout format similar to other national cup competitions around the world. Clubs from different tiers of Egyptian football, including the Egyptian Premier League and lower divisions, participate in the tournament. 2. **History:** The Egyptian Cup has a rich history dating back to its inception in 1921. It is one of the oldest football competitions in Egypt and has been a prestigious tournament throughout the country's footballing history. 3. **Participation:** A wide range of clubs participates in the Egyptian Cup each year, providing an opportunity for teams from lower divisions to compete against top-flight clubs. 4. **Winners:** Clubs like Al-Ahly and Zamalek have been dominant in the Egyptian Cup, frequently reaching the latter stages and winning multiple titles. Al-Ahly, in particular, has a strong record in this competition, adding to their extensive trophy cabinet. 5. **Significance:** Winning the Egyptian Cup not only brings prestige to the club but also often grants qualification to continental competitions such as the CAF Confederation Cup. Overall, the Egyptian Cup is an integral part of Egyptian football, providing clubs with the opportunity to compete for national glory and showcase their talent on a national stage.

Egyptian Premier League

Egyptian Premier League: Al-Ahly has won the Egyptian Premier League (formerly Egyptian League) a record 43 times as of my last update. They have been dominant in domestic football in Egypt for decades.

CAF Super Cup

CAF Super Cup: This is an annual African club competition where the winners of the CAF Champions League play against the winners of the CAF Confederation Cup. Al-Ahly has won this multiple times as well.
